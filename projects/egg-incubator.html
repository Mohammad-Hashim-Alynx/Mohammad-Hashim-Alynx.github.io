Programmable Automatic Egg Incubating Machine

Fully automated egg incubation system designed to maintain stable environmental conditions over a complete 28-day incubation cycle.

Project Overview

This project involved the design and deployment of a fully automated, industrial-grade egg incubation system built to address high chick mortality caused by inconsistent environmental control and labor-intensive manual monitoring in small to mid-scale poultry operations.

The system autonomously maintains precise temperature, humidity, ventilation, and egg-turning conditions throughout the complete 28-day incubation cycle, eliminating human intervention and ensuring consistent, repeatable hatch outcomes.

System Architecture & Subsystems

Temperature Control

Multi-level regulation using two W1209 digital thermostat controllers with K-type thermocouples. One controller manages the primary heating element, while the second provides redundant oversight, maintaining stability within ±0.5 °C of the 37.8 °C setpoint.

Humidity Management

A dedicated water reservoir with an ultrasonic mist maker controlled by a humidity sensing module. Control logic regulates misting duty cycle to maintain 55–65% RH, with programmable adjustments during final hatch phases.

Automatic Egg Turning Mechanism

An AC-powered swing motor with a programmable timer drives a camshaft connected to egg trays, rotating the tray assembly 45 degrees every four hours to prevent embryo adhesion and mimic natural hen behavior.

Ventilation & Air Circulation

High-volume DC axial fans establish forced-air circulation, ensuring uniform temperature and humidity distribution while maintaining adequate oxygen levels and CO₂ removal.

Power System

Primary AC operation with automatic failover to a 12 V DC battery system during mains failure, supported by an integrated charging controller for battery readiness.

Control Logic & Automation Strategy

The automation architecture follows a phased incubation state model consisting of pre-heat, main incubation, lockdown, and hatch stages. Each sensor–actuator pair operates in closed loop, supervised by central logic to ensure stability and fault tolerance.

Redundant temperature monitoring, continuous ventilation, and interval-based egg turning minimize single-point failures and environmental drift across the incubation cycle.

Power Failover & Reliability Engineering

A DPDT relay-based automatic AC–DC switching system ensures uninterrupted operation during power outages. When mains power is available, the system operates on rectified DC while charging a 12 V 100 Ah battery bank. Upon failure, the load is instantly transferred to battery power.

This design provides approximately three hours of uninterrupted operation, preventing temperature drops that could otherwise lead to embryo mortality.

Engineering Challenges & Solutions

  • Weather variation: Solved using a sealed, insulated enclosure with forced internal circulation.
  • Human error: Eliminated through full automation of control loops and egg turning.
  • Inconsistent monitoring: Resolved via continuous sensor-based supervision and autonomous corrective action.

Performance Outcomes & Impact

  • Zero human intervention required during the full 28-day cycle
  • Improved hatch consistency exceeding 85% under test conditions
  • Eliminated dedicated labor requirements
  • Multiple complete incubation cycles completed without failure

Technical Summary

  • Designed and built a fully automated incubation system integrating temperature, humidity, ventilation, and power-fail protection.
  • Implemented robust control logic using W1209 controllers and programmable electromechanical mechanisms.
  • Engineered an automatic AC–DC failover system guaranteeing uninterrupted operation during outages.
  • Validated performance across multiple full incubation cycles, achieving consistent hatch outcomes.
